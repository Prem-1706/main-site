What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to the long-term shifts in temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric conditions on Earth. While natural factors play a role, human activities—particularly the burning of fossil fuels—are accelerating these changes. This results in a variety of harmful environmental effects.

Global Effects of Climate Change

Climate change is already affecting our planet in profound ways. Here are some of the most significant global impacts:

Greenhouse Gases

The buildup of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, such as CO₂, methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O), is causing the planet to warm. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, preventing it from escaping into space.

Rising Temperatures

Global temperatures are rising, leading to heatwaves, droughts, and changing weather patterns. This impacts food production, water resources, and the health of ecosystems.

Environmental Challenges

Rising sea levels due to melting glaciers, habitat destruction, and extreme weather events are just a few of the many challenges posed by climate change. These affect wildlife, coastal communities, and our natural resources.

How Can You Contribute?

There are several actions you can take to reduce your carbon footprint and help combat climate change. Every effort counts!

  • Use energy-efficient appliances and switch to LED bulbs to reduce electricity consumption.
  • Choose renewable energy sources like solar or wind power if possible.
  • Drive less. Use public transport, walk, or bike to reduce your carbon emissions.
  • Eat more plant-based foods. The livestock industry contributes significantly to greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Plant trees and support reforestation efforts to help absorb CO₂.

Thank you for your time! Together, we can make a difference in the fight against climate change.
